Afore the Chaplet

On offering, anamnesis and belonging before praying the Divine Mercy Chaplet.

When I say, “Eternal Father, I offer you…”, I do not mean that Christ belongs to me, or that I repeat his sacrifice. The Son has already offered himself once for all. In the Eucharist, the Church sacramentally makes that one offering present; in this chaplet, I remember it, proclaim it and plead its mercy.

I speak as one baptised into Christ and numbered among his people. I am recipient before herald, carried before I speak. I bear witness to the gift already before the Father and ask that its mercy reach us and the whole world.

Christ takes my remembrance, my need and my consent into his own living intercession. Therefore, “I offer” means:

I confess the self-gift of your Son, place all my hope within it, and consent to be carried in his offering to the Father.

Christ makes the offering. I proclaim his gift, and he carries me within it.
