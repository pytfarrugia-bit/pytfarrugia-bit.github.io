Bio

Peter Marc Farrugia

Psychotherapist, theological researcher and writer from Malta.

Peter Marc Farrugia is a warranted Gestalt psychotherapist and a doctoral researcher in theology at St Mary’s University, London. His work brings Christian theology into sustained conversation with trauma, safeguarding, memory, symbolic practice and the moral limits of institutional closure.

His doctoral project, Safeguarding Time, examines how the Church may act decisively after abuse without claiming a completion that belongs neither to procedure nor to institutional self-description. The work draws especially on Holy Saturday, Marian and Johannine theology, sacramental realism and the distinction between necessary judgement and eschatological fulfilment.

He studied Divinity at the University of Cambridge and has further postgraduate training in conflict analysis and psychotherapy. Alongside academic writing and clinical practice, he develops devotional, visual and musical projects concerned with iconography, chant, the Little Office of the Blessed Virgin Mary and modal accompaniment on the mountain dulcimer.

His public work includes theological papers, writing on medieval literature and trauma, the interactive synoptic icon The Face and the Mother, and the 2026 solo exhibition Record of Nighttime, shown under the name Pietru Farrugia.
