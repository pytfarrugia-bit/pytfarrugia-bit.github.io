Mesomedes: Songs from Hadrian’s Court

Ancient Greek monodies in performative translation for voice and fretted dulcimer.

Methodological note: the Greek texts, pitches and measured rhythms remain the basis. The dulcimer is a translation instrument, not a reconstruction of the kithara or of a lost Roman sound. The hymns remain within their pagan sacred world; the project listens to the culture Christianity entered without retrospectively baptising it.

The project begins with the surviving notated monodies of Mesomedes of Crete. The Greek texts will be accompanied by singable English versions, solfège and practical measured notation.

Placed beside Officium Parvum, it approaches the same materials from the opposite direction. One gives measured form to a living Office; the other carries surviving ancient measurements across the silence of a lost performance tradition.

The first phase will include the two invocations and the hymns to Helios and Nemesis.
